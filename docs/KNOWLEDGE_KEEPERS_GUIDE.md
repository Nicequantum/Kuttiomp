# Knowledge Keepers Guide

*For Grandmother Comus, Grandfather, Sharente, and all who steward Narragansett language*

---

## Introduction

This guide is written for Knowledge Keepers — the elders, Two-Spirit stewards, parents, and clan members who carry Narragansett language and cultural knowledge. You do not need technical expertise to use Kuttiomp. This document explains what the platform does, how it protects your teachings, and how to participate with confidence.

---

## What Kuttiomp Does

Kuttiomp is a **gathering place for language** — a digital home where:

1. **Words are preserved** with their cultural meaning, not just English translations
2. **Voices are recorded** and permanently linked to the person who spoke
3. **Knowledge flows** through the same generational structure your family has always used
4. **Sacred teachings** are protected from public access

Kuttiomp does **not** replace you. It amplifies your voice so learners today and generations from now can hear *your* pronunciation, *your* teaching, *your* cultural context.

## Your Role in the System

### If You Are an Elder (Grandmother Comus, Grandfather)

You hold **approval authority** over:
- Sacred and ceremonial content
- Content marked for elder review
- Final publication of restricted teachings

Your voice recordings are marked as **primary references** for learners. When you record a word, that recording becomes the authoritative pronunciation for that entry.

### If You Are Sharente (Two-Spirit)

You steward:
- Inclusive kinship terminology
- Gender-inclusive language evolution
- Cultural bridge between traditional and contemporary understanding

The platform recognizes your role explicitly. Content you create is tagged with Sharente attribution and may have specific visibility settings you control.

### If You Are a Parent or Sibling

You contribute:
- Daily life vocabulary
- Conversational practice recordings
- Teaching domain expertise (food, outdoors, crafts, etc.)

Your recordings are attributed to you and enter the approval queue. Elders review before public publication.

### If You Are a Clan Member

You extend the knowledge network with:
- Specialized vocabulary (fishing, songs, gathering)
- Stories and oral traditions
- Regional place names and local knowledge

## How to Record Audio

### Step-by-Step

1. Open the **Audio Studio** in the admin dashboard
2. **Select the speaker** — this must be the person whose voice is being recorded
3. Add **recording context** (e.g., "kitchen conversation," "formal lesson")
4. Press **Start Recording** and speak clearly
5. Press **Stop Recording** and review the playback
6. Press **Upload with Attribution**

### Important Rules

- **Never attribute someone else's voice to yourself**
- **Never record ceremonial content** without elder authorization
- **Always identify the speaker** — even if you pressed the record button for someone else (use "recorded by" field)
- **Practice sessions** are marked differently from formal teachings

## How to Add Words

When adding a lexical entry:

| Field | What to Include |
|-------|----------------|
| Narragansett word | The word as you write/spell it |
| English gloss | Brief translation (not a full explanation) |
| Cultural context | *When* and *how* the word is used — this is the most important field |
| Category | Noun, verb, kinship term, ceremonial, etc. |
| Primary speaker | Who taught you this word |
| Visibility | Who should be able to see this |

### Visibility Guide

- **Public** — Greetings, common words safe for any learner
- **Clan** — Family and clan knowledge (default)
- **Family** — Immediate family teachings
- **Elders Only** — Requires elder review before any access
- **Sacred** — Ceremonial knowledge (restricted)

When in doubt, choose **Clan** or **Family**. Elders can always broaden access later.

## Approval Process

### What Happens After You Submit

1. Your content enters the system with status **Pending**
2. Sacred content automatically goes to **Requires Elder Review**
3. An elder or authorized keeper reviews in the **Approvals** section
4. Upon approval, content becomes available at its designated visibility level
5. All actions are logged in the audit trail

### Your Rights

- You can request removal of your recordings at any time
- You can change visibility restrictions on content you authored
- You can add pronunciation variants if your dialect differs from another speaker's

## What the AI Does (and Does Not Do)

The AI assistant helps **learners** with:
- Translation suggestions
- Etymology research pointers
- Pronunciation guidance (IPA)
- Cultural context explanations

The AI **does not**:
- Replace your authority as a Knowledge Keeper
- Generate ceremonial or sacred content
- Publish anything without human review
- Store your private conversations for training

All AI interactions are logged and can be reviewed by elders.
